01 — Brand Culture

What We Stand For

“Golf was never meant to be exclusive. It was meant to be a walk in nature with intention. The Green Standard exists for those who believe the fairway is a place for everyone — a space to breathe, connect, and carry yourself with quiet confidence.”

We are not a golf brand. We are a culture.

The Green Standard was born from a simple truth: the best moments on a course have nothing to do with your handicap. They're in the morning mist on the first tee. The silence before a putt. The walk between holes with someone you haven't seen in months. The drive home with the windows down.

We believe golf is a form of meditation disguised as a sport. It teaches you patience. Presence. The beauty of showing up, even when your game doesn't. We're here for the weekend warriors. The ones who play 9 after work just to decompress. The friend groups who book a tee time instead of a bar table. The people who never grew up with golf but felt something pull them toward the green.

Our products don't scream. They speak quietly. Vintage textures meet modern restraint. Every piece carries a sense of history while belonging completely to now. Clean lines. Muted earth tones. Materials that age with character.

This is golf for the culture. Accessible, peaceful, and always with standard.

Peace

Golf is meditation in motion. We design for the calm, not the competition.

Connection

The course is where friendships deepen. We're for the walk, the conversation, the shared silence.

Simplicity

Strip away the excess. What remains is clean, intentional, and timeless.

Standard

Accessible doesn't mean ordinary. We hold ourselves — and our community — to a higher standard of care.

04 — Marketing Strategy

Selling Culture, Not Products

The Green Standard doesn't market accessories. It markets a feeling. Every piece of content should make someone think: "I want that life." The product is the entry point into the culture.

Content Pillars

01

The Peaceful Game

Cinematic, slow-paced content showing golf as meditation. Morning light, empty courses, close-ups of dew on grass. No scoreboards. No competition. Just presence.

02

The Culture

People from all walks of life — different ages, genders, backgrounds — enjoying golf their way. Weekend rounds, after-work 9s, friends connecting. Golf as social ritual.

03

The Craft

Close-up product shots. Leather textures, stitching details, the way canvas ages. Show the materials, the design intention. Let the products speak through their quality.

04

The Walk

Behind-the-scenes of building The Green Standard. Your journey, sourcing trips, design decisions, community stories. Raw, honest, invite people into the brand being built.

TikTok Strategy

TikTok is your discovery engine. This is where new golfers and streetwear-curious audiences find you. The algorithm favours mood and authenticity over polish.

Content Types That Convert

  • "Golf isn't what you think" series — 15-30s clips reframing golf as peaceful, accessible, and stylish. Hook: "Golf is the most underrated form of self-care." Show cinematic B-roll of morning rounds.
  • Product reveal / unboxing — ASMR-style. Unwrapping waxed canvas, the sound of a leather zipper, placing items in the carry bag. Slow. Intentional. No voiceover needed, just ambient course sounds.
  • "What I bring to the course" — Lay-flat styling of your products with a peaceful aesthetic. Think Japanese knolling videos. Everything placed with precision on a wooden surface or linen backdrop.
  • "Golf for the culture" series — Real people, diverse, showing up to golf in their way. Not polished athletes — friends, couples, solo players. The anti-country-club vibe.
  • Build-in-public — Show the journey. Picking fabrics, sampling from manufacturers, designing the brand. This builds community investment before the first sale.

Posting Cadence

  • 4–5 posts per week during pre-launch (build anticipation)
  • Daily during launch week (first 7 days)
  • 3–4 per week ongoing (sustainable rhythm)

Sound & Aesthetic

  • Lo-fi Japanese beats, ambient nature sounds, or trending peaceful audio
  • Muted colour grading — slightly desaturated, warm shadows
  • Slow motion B-roll mixed with handheld "real" footage
  • Minimal text overlays in Inter or similar clean sans-serif

Hashtag Strategy

  • #GolfTok #GolfCulture #GolfStyle #WeekendGolf
  • #Minimalism #SlowLiving #PeacefulGame
  • #TheGreenStandard (branded)
  • #GolfIsForEveryone #SmartStreetWear

Instagram Strategy

Instagram is your brand home. This is where the aesthetic lives permanently. Your grid is your storefront, your stories are your personality, and reels drive reach.

Grid Aesthetic

  • Alternating between product shots, lifestyle/course photography, and text-based culture quotes
  • Muted, consistent colour palette — sage, stone, cream, charcoal
  • Every 3rd post should be a "culture moment" — a quote, a philosophy, a question
  • No busy graphics. White space is your friend. Think Aesop, Muji, or COS visual language.

Reels (Discovery)

  • Repurpose top-performing TikToks (crosspost with slight edits)
  • "One product, one story" — 30s mini-films for each product showing it in context on the course
  • Transition videos — streetwear outfit to course-ready in one smooth cut. Show the crossover appeal.

Stories (Community)

  • Weekly "Sunday Session" Q&A about golf, the brand, or culture
  • Polls and "this or that" to involve the community in product decisions
  • Repost customer content (UGC is critical once live)
  • Behind-the-scenes of sampling, packaging design, photoshoots

Influencer Strategy

  • Micro-influencers (5K–50K) — Golf lifestyle creators who aren't traditional "golf bros." Look for creators who blend golf with fashion, travel, or wellness.
  • Gifting programme — Send "The Standard Kit" bundle to 20–30 creators at launch. No paid posts — just genuine product placement. This works because the products photograph beautifully.
  • Streetwear crossover creators — Reach beyond golf. Target fashion/lifestyle creators who'd appreciate the aesthetic even without being golfers.

Posting Cadence

  • Feed: 3–4 posts per week
  • Stories: Daily (minimum)
  • Reels: 2–3 per week

12-Week Launch Roadmap

Weeks 1–3

Foundation

Finalise brand identity (logo, typography, colour system). Set up Instagram and TikTok accounts. Begin "build in public" content. Order samples from HIBO. Secure domain and build a simple Shopify landing page with email capture: "Something peaceful is coming."

Weeks 4–6

Content Engine

Shoot product photography with samples (flat lays, on-course, lifestyle). Create 20+ pieces of content for both platforms. Begin posting 4–5x/week on TikTok, 3x/week on Instagram. Tease products without revealing everything. Build the email list to 500+ through social and a referral incentive.

Weeks 7–8

Community & Hype

Launch a "Founding Members" waitlist with early access pricing. Send gifting packages to 20–30 micro-influencers. Run a countdown on stories. Drop one product teaser per day in the final week. Place your bulk order with HIBO (100 units each product).

Weeks 9–10

Launch

Open the store. Email blast to waitlist with 48-hour early access. Post daily on both platforms. Highlight The Standard Kit bundle as the "starter culture pack." Engage every comment and DM personally. Run a small TikTok Spark Ads budget (€20–25/day) on your best-performing organic content.

Weeks 11–12

Optimise & Scale

Analyse what sold, what content performed, and where traffic came from. Double down on top performers. Collect and reshare UGC. Plan your second drop (seasonal colourway, limited edition headcover, or the first clothing piece). Begin building the community for long-term retention.

Additional Growth Channels

Pinterest

Your moodboard energy lives here. Pin product flatlays, course photography, and lifestyle imagery. Pinterest drives long-tail organic traffic to Shopify. Create boards like "Morning Round Essentials," "Vintage Golf Aesthetic," and "The Peaceful Game."

Email / SMS

Build from day one. Use Klaviyo or Mailchimp. Welcome sequence, abandoned cart recovery, and a weekly "Sunday Standard" newsletter with course recommendations, product care tips, and culture stories. SMS for drops and restocks only — keep it rare and valuable.

05 — Your First Video

The Origin Story

This is the most important piece of content you'll ever make. It's not a product video. It's a culture video. It tells people who you are, why this exists, and why they should care. Raw, honest, no script teleprompter — just you talking to the camera like you're talking to a mate.

Video 1 — "Why I Started The Green Standard"

Format: 60–90 seconds • Filmed handheld or on tripod • On a course at golden hour or in a quiet, clean space • Muted tones, natural light

Opening Hook (0–3 seconds)

"Golf has a gatekeeping problem. And I want to change that."

Straight to camera. No intro, no logo, no music yet. Just you and the statement. This stops the scroll.

The Honest Story (3–30 seconds)

Speak naturally. Something like:

"I'm just a regular guy. I don't come from money. I didn't grow up at a country club. But when I first stepped onto a golf course, something clicked. It wasn't about the score. It was the walk. The quiet. The feeling of being completely present. And I thought — why does this feel like it's not for me? Why does golf feel like a members-only club when all it really is... is a peaceful walk with a purpose?"

The Mission (30–50 seconds)

"So I created The Green Standard. Not a golf brand — a culture. For people who see golf the way I do. It's not about competition. It's about connection. Weekend rounds with your mates. A 9-hole reset after a long week. Looking clean on the course without spending a fortune. Golf should be accessible. And the gear should match that energy — minimal, vintage-inspired, affordable. No elitism. Just standard."

The Transparency (50–70 seconds)

"I'm going to be transparent with you. I built this on my own. My funds, my time, late nights with AI tools helping me figure out what I couldn't, my close friends and family backing me up. I didn't have a blueprint for this. I just had a feeling that golf needed something different. And if you're watching this, you probably feel it too."

The Invite (70–90 seconds)

"So if you believe golf is for everyone — follow the journey. First drop is coming. And it's going to set the standard."

Production Notes

  • B-roll intercuts: Close-ups of the products (leather texture, stitching, the carry bag on grass), empty fairways at sunrise, your hands holding a club, walking a course alone
  • Music: Lo-fi ambient — something calm and cinematic, builds slightly toward the end
  • Text overlay at the end: "THE GREEN STANDARD" in Cormorant Garamond, sage green on black, with your handle underneath
  • No hard sell. No "link in bio." The story IS the marketing. Let people find you.
  • Post simultaneously on TikTok (full), Instagram Reels (full), and Instagram feed (30s cut with carousel stills)
